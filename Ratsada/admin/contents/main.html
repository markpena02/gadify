GAD System Overview

This system is a knowledge management platform designed for the Gender and Development (GAD) Office at USeP. It uses the Harmonized Gender and Development Guidelines (HGDG) and Gender Mainstreaming Evaluation Framework (GMEF) tools.

All proposals, projects, and research requests must undergo a GAD evaluation to ensure they meet GAD criteria before being approved.

System Users

  • System Admin: This role is managed by the Chair and allows the admin to oversee all system activities. The admin can create, delete, and edit user accounts and ensure the system's integrity.
  • Evaluators: A committee of four members, appointed by the university president, reviews proposals to assess their "gender responsiveness." Each proposal is evaluated by one member, but all must agree on the assessment before a decision is sent to the proposer. Evaluators can access proposers' documents and provide feedback on their evaluations.
  • Proposers: University staff or department heads submit proposals for projects, research, or other requests to the GAD Office for approval. Proposers use the system to submit their proposals and supporting documents and track their submissions. They receive feedback and decision letters through the system.

Tools Used

  • HGDG Tool: The HGDG tool helps evaluate projects for gender responsiveness. It includes a checklist that the evaluators use to rate projects based on how well they consider the needs of all genders.
  • GMEF Tool: The GMEF tool serves as a file archiving system for storing project files and other documentation related to GAD Office work. These records provide evidence of the university's gender-responsive practices and are used for audits every three years.
